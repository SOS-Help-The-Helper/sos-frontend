Signal
Maria texts from WhatsApp:
"My house flooded. I have two kids. We need shelter."
She could have texted from iMessage, called a hotline, posted in a Slack channel, or filled out a web form. The channel doesn't matter. SOS meets people where they are.
What enters the system: Location (Burnsville, NC), household size (4), urgency (critical), category (housing), vulnerability (children). Extracted conversationally — no forms.
Intelligence
12 organizations evaluated.
Emergency RV selected. Score: 95 out of 100.
The matching engine scores every available partner across proximity, capability, urgency, capacity, and trust — in under 10 seconds. But it doesn't just match. It explains.
The decision trace: "ERV selected: 3.1 mi, housing capability, capacity for 6, trust score 88. Runners-up: Red Cross (7.2 mi, score 74), Community Church (2.1 mi, score 61, low capacity)." Ask why any match was made — the answer is in the trace.
Graph
Maria → ERV for housing.
ERV → Free Hot Meals for food.
Two orgs connected for the first time.
ERV housed Maria's family. Then Woody said: "They need food too." SOS connected ERV to Free Hot Meals — an organization they'd never coordinated with before. That relationship now exists in the graph.
What compounds: Next disaster, ERV and Free Hot Meals are already connected. The system knows they work well together. Multi-org coordination that used to require phone calls now happens automatically.
Network
ERV delivered an RV in 45 minutes.
Trust score: 88 → 93.
Trust isn't assumed from credentials or org size. It's earned through outcomes. ERV responded fast and delivered. That record means they'll match first for the next critical housing need.
Everyone is a helper: Maria, once sheltered, helped her neighbor evacuate. Her trust score went up too. A displaced family with a truck became a transport resource. Needs and offers coexist on the same person.
Adaptive
ERV: 6 RVs → 5 available.
Library food site: 43 of 83 meals remaining.
Route 19 bridge: closed.
The entire network's state updates in real-time. Capacity, conditions, availability — the next match already knows what changed. No one has to radio in a status update.
Why this matters: The next family matched to ERV gets the right RV for their size. The food match routes to the site with capacity. The transport avoids the closed bridge. Real-time state eliminates wasted effort.
Learning
"Shelter matches within 5 miles fulfill 3× faster."
"Families with children under 5 need 40% more follow-up."
"Food matches after 2pm should route to dinner sites."
Every match, every outcome, every decision feeds the learning layer. Patterns emerge that no human could track across thousands of coordination events. Weights adjust. The next disaster starts with intelligence this one didn't have.
The moat: A competitor can copy the code. They can't copy 10,000 decision traces with outcomes, across multiple disaster types, spanning years of compounding intelligence. The architecture is reproducible. The intelligence is not.
