Coordination Infrastructure

The help exists.
The coordination doesn't.

After 18 months of field research across five major disasters, we identified a structural gap in how communities, agencies, and organizations coordinate relief. SOS is the infrastructure layer designed to close it.

The Problem

A convergence nobody's addressing.

305M
people needed humanitarian assistance in 2025
68%
of those identified didn't receive help
$24B
funding shortfall — the largest on record

Federal capacity is contracting

79% of state emergency management agencies want AI-powered coordination tools. None have them. Federal disaster funding dropped 40% in the first half of 2025.

AI can now coordinate at scale

Conversational intake, real-time resource matching, and multi-channel coordination are feasible today. None of this was possible two years ago.

Communities are the actual first responders

85% of disaster survivors receive help from neighbors before any agency arrives. The largest coordination network already exists — it just has no infrastructure.

The Thesis

A displaced family with a truck clears roads.
A nurse in a flood zone triages injuries.
A teenager who speaks three languages runs comms.

Everyone is a helper.

Needs and offers coexist on the same person. The public isn't a liability in disaster response — they are the response. What they lack is coordination infrastructure.

The Solution

How SOS coordinates.

Six layers of coordination intelligence. Each builds on the last. We call the framework SIGNAL.

S

Signal

Maria texts: "My house flooded. Two kids. We need shelter and food."
Any channel — SMS, WhatsApp, web. AI extracts structured needs from a conversation.
I

Intelligence

Available partners scored. Shelter matched within radius. Food bank notified automatically.
Two needs, two partners, one coordinated response. Every decision traced and auditable.
G

Graph

At the shelter, the system surfaces: "Translation services needed nearby." Maria speaks three languages.
SOS sees what people can offer — not just what they need. Connections form in real time.
N

Network

Maria helps three families who only speak Creole. A survivor becomes a helper.
Trust is earned through verified outcomes. Contribution scores compound over time.
A

Adaptive

Shelter capacity, meals served, translation queue — all updated across the network in real time.
Every match already knows what changed. No stale data.
L

Learning

"Bilingual citizens resolve intakes 40% faster." The system surfaced that pattern.
Every disaster makes the next response smarter. The intelligence compounds.

Maria found shelter in 45 minutes. Then helped three families.
The system learned from all of it.
